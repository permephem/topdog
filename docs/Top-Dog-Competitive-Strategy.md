## Executive Summary

Top Dog is not losing on quality — your Google reviews are 5.0 stars and customers consistently praise honest pricing, Power Stroke expertise, and getting trucks back when it matters. The opportunity is to **out-trust** Billings competitors by turning that reputation into visibility, faster lead response, and a repeatable promise: **realistic finish dates and progress updates on every job.**

This plan is organized into three 4-week phases over 90 days. No big ad spend required — the highest-return moves are reviews, Google Business Profile activity, fleet outreach, and making the shop operation match what the website already promises.

**Position to own:**  
*Billings' honest Power Stroke and work-truck shop — you get a finish date, progress updates, and no BS.*

## Phase 1: Close Critical Gaps (Weeks 1–4)

### 1. Win the review race — highest ROI

Competitors outrank you on **volume**, not quality. A 5.0 with 7 reviews often loses to a 4.8 with 40 in local search.

**Actions:**
- After every satisfied job (especially injectors, heads, fleet work), send a direct Google review link within 24 hours
- Respond to every existing review on Google Business Profile
- **Target: 30 reviews in 90 days** (~2–3 per week)
- Ask fleet customers and 6.0 owners specifically — they write the reviews you want

### 2. Make the website capture leads

The current GitHub Pages preview is a brochure only. To activate the **callback form**, **SMS alerts**, and **shop TV display**, the site needs to run on a server host (e.g. Vercel free tier) with email and text notification services configured.

**Actions:**
- Deploy the live site to Vercel (or similar) as the primary customer-facing URL
- Set up email notifications (Resend) and optional SMS alerts (Twilio) for callback requests
- Consider a custom domain (e.g. topdogautodiesel.com) for customer trust
- Add real bay photos to the website gallery

### 3. Never lose a lead while under the hood

| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| **Shop TV display** | Full-screen alerts + audible chime on a monitor in the bay |
| **SMS alerts** | Backup notification to your phone when away from the desk |
| **Callback form** | Captures leads when the phone goes unanswered |

**Actions:**
- Connect a shop monitor to your laptop; keep the display page open during shop hours
- Update voicemail: *"Under the hood — leave a message or request a callback at [website URL]"*
- Consider Google Voice for missed-call text transcription on the shop line

### 4. Operationalize progress updates

Customers care about **when it's done** and **what changed** — not live shop cameras. This is your version of high-end build transparency, scaled for a working diesel shop.

**Simple shop routine (30 seconds between jobs):**

1. **Check-in** — "Diagnosis + finish estimate by [day/time]"
2. **After diagnosis** — Written estimate + target completion date
3. **Mid-job (major work only)** — Text if scope or timeline changes; photo optional
4. **If delayed** — Customer hears from you first, not the other way around

This matches what the website already promises in the Good to Know and FAQ sections.

## Phase 2: Win Visibility (Weeks 5–8)

### 5. Dominate Google Business Profile

For local repair, GBP often matters more than website SEO.

**Actions:**
- Complete every field: services (Power Stroke, 6.0, Cummins, Duramax, fleet), hours, photos
- Post 1–2 photos per week: bay work, completed jobs, team
- Add Q&A entries: walk-ins, estimates, timelines, fleet service
- Keep name, address, phone consistent everywhere: **6855 Niehenke Ave, Billings, MT 59101**

### 6. Own diesel search terms

Target phrases customers actually search:

- Power Stroke repair Billings
- 6.0 Power Stroke Billings
- diesel mechanic Billings MT
- fleet diesel repair Billings

**Actions:**
- List the shop on Bing Places, Apple Maps, and Yelp (same address and phone everywhere)
- Encourage happy customers to mention "Power Stroke" or "diesel" naturally in reviews
- Optional later: dedicated Power Stroke landing page if search data supports it

### 7. Fleet and contractor outreach

One contractor with five trucks beats dozens of one-off oil changes.

**Actions:**
- Identify 10 local fleets, ranchers, or contractors
- Offer priority scheduling + timeline updates + fleet billing
- Use existing customer stories in outreach (e.g. two trucks down, both back on the road)

### 8. Social proof content

**Actions:**
- Post 2–3 times per week on Instagram/Facebook: bay photos, "what we found" stories, completed jobs
- Refresh website gallery with real shop photography
- Repurpose strong review quotes on social media

## What Not to Do

- **Live shop cameras** — privacy, liability, and distraction; progress texts work better for repair shops
- **Race to cheapest oil change** — commoditized; competitors cannot match diesel depth plus honesty
- **Performance mod marketing** — FM Performance and Diesel Schopp own that lane
- **Copy competitors' long town-list SEO** — one strong Billings + Yellowstone County message is enough
- **Promise timelines you cannot keep** — one missed date erases ten good reviews
